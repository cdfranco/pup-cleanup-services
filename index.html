Pet Health & Safety

Dog waste left in your yard can be a health hazard to dogs in the following ways:

  • Pathogens: Dog waste carries pathogens like Salmonella & E. coli, which can cause food poisoning in dogs if ingested.
  • Parasites: Dog waste can harbor parasites such as roundworms, hookworms, and giardia.
  • Toxic Mold and Fungi: Dog waste is rich in nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus, which are essential for the growth of mold and fungi.
  • Rodents: Dog waste can attract rodents to your yard which can carry harmful diseases and ruin the health of your lawn.

Clean Home & Family Health

Your dog's waste gets tracked into your home if it gets left in your yard. This poses several health risks for your family:

  • E. coli and Salmonella: Dog feces contain bacteria like E. coli and Salmonella, which can cause food poisoning in humans.
  • Parvovirus: This highly contagious virus can survive on surfaces for extended periods.
  • Roundworms and Hookworms: These parasites can be transmitted to humans, especially children.
  • Allergic Reactions: Dog feces can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals.

Fresh Community

Responsibly managing your dog's waste is important for the health & cleanliness of the community that we all love & enjoy.

  • Clean Waterways: Pet waste left on the street or lawn doesn't just go away or fertilize the grass.
  • Clean Air: Dog waste can release bacteria into the air, particularly in dry, windy conditions.

Lawn Health

Dog waste left on your lawn can cause several problems:

  • Nitrogen Burn: Dog waste is high in nitrogen, a nutrient essential for plant growth.
  • Spotty Lawn: The areas around the waste will often be the most affected.
  • Fungal Growth: Dog waste creates a moist environment that is ideal for fungal growth.
  • Attracts Pests & Rodents: Pet waste attracts insects & rodents that can create nests in your grass.
